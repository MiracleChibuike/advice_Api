"Still I Rise"

Today is Black Poetry Day in the United States, a chance to celebrate the
rich tradition of African American writers of verse. While many people are familiar
with names such as Langston Hughes and Maya Angelou, the history and
ongoing story of Black American poetry features a diverse canon of powerful
voices, including James Weldon Johnson, Countee Cullen, Amiri Baraka, Audre Lorde,
Lucille Clifton, Nikki Giovanni, Rita Dove, Alice Walker, and Kevin Young.
