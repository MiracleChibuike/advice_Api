Deftly Death-Defying

The first flying trapeze act was performed 165 years ago today by its inventor, Jules Léotard, without a net, in Paris. Léotard, immortalized as “the daring young man on the flying trapeze,” also lent his name to the one-piece garment he popularized. Léotard’s feats of daring—and those of Frenchman Blondin—stoked an interest in high-wire acrobatics, a thrilling art form that found purchase in circuses, gave Harry Houdini his start, and continues to delight via Cirque du Soleil.
